HONORS 100

Where it all started...

Autumn 2018

Creating my four year course plan, though it was tentative, really got me to think more clearly about what I want to do in life and how I am going to get there. I found by doing my plan that I have more flexibility in my timeline than I originally thought, meaning that I have time to pursue things like research, study abroad, or potentially a minor. I started to explore these options and found that the things I was getting most excited about were the neural applications of computer science, such as research at the Integrated Brain Imaging Center and the Neural Computation and Engineering minor. The application of my CS degree in a career was always something that I was unsure about, but now I think that I have at least found a starting point for another passion that can direct my career. Hopefully within the next year I will be able to try out some of the classes in the NCE minor and decide if that is something I want to continue to pursue.

HONORS 392

Science and Engineering for Social Justice

Autumn 2019

Going into this past quarter I was the least excited about my honors class. I had only signed up for it because there was space in it and it fit my schedule. However, this turned out to be one of my favorite classes this quarter. The topic of the class, Science and Engineering for Social Justice, is exactly what I was interested after my time in Rome learning about the European Refugee Crisis. After the summer I felt inspired to use my life to make more of a difference, but was at a loss for how with a STEM degree. This honors class helped inspire me and reinvigorated my commitment to my major. After taking this class I've realized that there is so much I can do to help people by using a STEM degree, and I've realized how important honors classes are to me. Even the courses I'm not thrilled about going into end up having something important to offer. Moving forward I'm pretty sure that I will stay in Computer Science, but I'm searching now for an application that makes a positive impact and I know I can find one. This search has particularly transferred over into what CS electives I'm taking and what internships I have been applying to. HONORS 240

Publication and Exhibition Making in Contemporary Art

Autumn 2020

This class was really pivotal for me since it reignited my passion for the fine arts. This was the first course in college where I was pushed to be purely creative and make a complete product from my own creativity. I was very proud of my final project and am very happy to share it here as both a component of the class exhibition and a standalone piece. This class also inspired me to register next quarter for the Intro Sculpture class where I'm excited to explore new mediums.
